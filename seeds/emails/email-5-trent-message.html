Hi,

I started Cyber Safety Project because I spent years as a teacher watching students navigate an online world their schools weren't equipped to talk about. A lot has changed since then. Some of it is remarkable. Some of it genuinely worries me.

Here are five things I'm seeing right now that every school leader needs to be thinking about before 2027 begins.

1

AI is in the classroom whether schools are ready or not.

Students are using generative AI tools daily. Most have had no guidance on how to do so safely, ethically, or honestly. Schools that don't address this in 2027 will be managing the consequences in 2028.
2

Group chats are the number one source of school-based conflict.

What happens in a group chat at 10pm lands on a teacher's desk at 9am. Without a shared language for digital behaviour, schools are responding to crises instead of preventing them.
3

Deepfakes and AI-generated content are reaching primary school students.

This is no longer a secondary school issue. Year 4 students are encountering manipulated images and videos and have no framework for questioning what they see online.
4

Teacher confidence is not keeping pace with student risk.

Most teachers want to address online safety in their classrooms. Very few feel equipped to do it well. The gap between intent and capability is where incidents happen.
5

One-off programs are not enough anymore.

The digital landscape changes faster than an annual incursion can address it. The schools seeing the best outcomes are the ones with a consistent, embedded curriculum that builds knowledge year on year.

None of these are problems you can solve with a single assembly or a permission slip home. They need a program. And that program needs to start early in the year, with every class, built into how your school teaches.

That is exactly what Cyber Safe Classroom is designed to do.
