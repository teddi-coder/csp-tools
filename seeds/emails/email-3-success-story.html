Northcross Christian School wanted online safety to be part of how every teacher taught. Not a one-off incursion. Not someone else's responsibility.

Before Cyber Safe Classroom, cyber safety education at Northcross relied on occasional external sessions. Students got the message once a year, if they were lucky. Teachers weren't confident addressing digital issues as they came up. And leadership had no clear picture of what was being taught, or when.

With Cyber Safe Classroom embedded across Foundation to Year 8, that changed. Every teacher had the tools to lead a lesson. Every student received consistent, age-appropriate content throughout the year. And leadership could see exactly what was being delivered and when.

“The whole-school approach has been transformative. Our staff feel equipped, our students are having real conversations, and parents can see we take this seriously.”

Leadership team, Northcross Christian School

What they achieved

A consistent program across every year level

Every class, Foundation to Year 8, receiving sequenced online safety lessons throughout the year.

Teachers who felt confident, not caught out

Ready-to-deliver lessons meant staff could lead discussions on digital citizenship without needing to be tech experts.

A community that could see the commitment

Parents and the broader school community had visible proof that online safety was embedded, not an afterthought.
